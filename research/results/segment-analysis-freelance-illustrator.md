## Key Insights

### Insight 1: The "Antivirus for Art" Concept
**3/5 interviewers independently used the antivirus metaphor.**
- "Like antivirus for art. You install it and forget about it."
- "I want a notification: 'Found and removed.' That's it."
- "Install and forget" — this is the UX model artists want.

**Implication:** CVBER should position as "set it and forget it" protection. No dashboard. No reports. Just notifications when something is found and handled.

### Insight 2: Trust Through Transparency
**4/5 demanded to understand the business model before trusting.**
- "How is it free? What's the catch?"
- "If I'm not paying, they're selling my data."
- "Show me the team, the tech, the business model."

**Implication:** CVBER needs a public business model page, open-source core tech, and team credentials visible.

### Insight 3: The Proof Threshold
**5/5 required proof before committing.**
- "Show me one artist who used it and got their art taken down."
- "I need to use it for 3-6 months before recommending."
- "A success story from someone I follow — not a paid promotion."

**Implication:** CVBER needs a "proof wall" — real case studies from real artists before mass marketing.

### Insight 4: Global vs Western-Centric
**1/5 explicitly called out non-Western needs, but all non-US personas implicitly needed global protection.**
- "If someone in China steals my art, what am I going to do?"
- "The system is designed for rich Western artists."
- "Protection needs to be global too."

**Implication:** CVBER's global enforcement (especially for non-US artists) is a major differentiator.

### Insight 5: Business vs Art Framing
**1/5 explicitly said: "Frame as a business tool, not an art tool."**
- "I'm not protecting my art — I'm protecting my business."
- "Every incident costs me $300+ in time."
- "A tool that saves me 10 hours/month is worth $750/year."

**Implication:** CVBER should market to the business side of freelancing, not just the creative side.

## Product-Market Fit Verdict

### ✅ STRONG FIT
**Problem severity is very high (9.6/10).** Artists are desperate for a solution.

### ⚠️ CONDITIONAL FIT
**Adoption depends on:**
1. Proof it works (real case studies)
2. Transparent business model
3. Effortless UX (antivirus model)
4. Global enforcement capability
