## Executive Summary

CVBER addresses a $25 billion annual problem — digital art theft — with a free, open-source, AI-powered platform that detects stolen work, proves ownership, and enforces rights. The global digital art market reached $6.69B in 2026 (growing at 14.66% CAGR), the creator economy surpasses $314B, and AI-generated copyright infringement has triggered 70+ active lawsuits with $50B+ in cumulative claimed damages. Despite this crisis, existing solutions are fragmented, expensive, and reactive. CVBER is the first platform to unify detection, provenance, and enforcement in a single free tool — positioned to capture the 95% of artists who cannot afford current protection services.

## 3. Competitive Analysis

### Competitive Landscape Overview

The copyright protection market is fragmented across three categories: image monitoring services, DMCA enforcement platforms, and emerging provenance standards. No single player offers the integrated Detect → Prove → Enforce pipeline CVBER provides.

### Competitor Deep Dive

#### Pixsy

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Founded | 2014 |
| Pricing | Free (500 images), $19/mo (2K), $39/mo (30K), $89/mo (100K) |
| Commission | 50% of recovered damages |
| Images monitored | 150M+ daily |
| Matches found | 350M+ cumulative |
| Strengths | Large scale, user-friendly UI, US Copyright Office integration, global legal network |
| Weaknesses | **50% commission is aggressive**; free tier limited to 500 images; no C2PA/blockchain; no proactive monitoring (reactive only); slow back-office (user reports); strong in US, weaker internationally |
| Target market | Professional photographers |

| Attribute | Detail |

The legal system is catching up. Artists need tools to prove ownership and enforce rights NOW — before, during, and after litigation.
