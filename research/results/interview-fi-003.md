## INTERVIEW

### Q1: Tell me about your art. What do you create and where do you share it?

**Priya:** I do character illustration — mostly anime-inspired fantasy characters. I have about 4K followers on Instagram and I'm building up my Twitter. I don't do commissions yet because I'm still building my portfolio. I'm trying to break into the industry — maybe concept art for games or animation. Right now I'm doing it full-time but the money is tight. Some months I make $2,000, some months I make $800.

### Q2: How did you get into this? What keeps you going?

**Priya:** I studied fine arts in college but I was always drawing characters. I started posting on Instagram two years ago and people liked my work. I thought maybe I could make a career of it. What keeps me going is... I see other artists at my level who made it. They got clients, they got recognition. If they can do it, maybe I can too. But honestly? I'm scared. The market is getting harder. AI is getting better. And I see artists getting their work stolen constantly on Twitter. It's like... why would I keep creating if someone can just take it?

### Q3: What's the best thing that's happened with your art this year?

**Priya:** I got featured on a popular art account on Instagram. Gained 800 followers in a week. Got three commission inquiries. It felt like my break. But then I saw other artists talking about how featured accounts sometimes steal the art and repost without credit. I checked — they credited me, but still. The anxiety of wondering whether your work is safe even when good things happen... it's exhausting.

### Q4: Has anyone ever used your work without permission? Tell me what happened.

**Priya:** Not yet. But I've seen it happen to artists I follow. @artbyleah had her entire portfolio reposted on a Chinese site. @joshdraws had his character designs sold on Redbubble without permission. These are artists I look up to. If it happens to them, it'll happen to me. I'm just waiting for it. And when it does, I have no idea what to do. I can't afford a lawyer. I can't afford to file anything. I'll just... have to deal with it.

### Q5: How did that make you feel? What did you do about it?

**Priya:** Watching it happen to others makes me feel helpless. Like, I should do something now to prevent it. But what? I don't have money for protection. I don't have knowledge of the legal system. I'm in India — the laws here are different from the US. If someone in China steals my art, what am I going to do? Sue them in Chinese court? I can't even afford to fly there. The whole system feels like it's designed for rich Western artists.

### Q6: What do you worry about when it comes to your art being stolen or copied?

**Priya:** Everything. I worry about the moment I get successful enough to be a target. Right now I'm too small to matter. But if I get to 50K followers, if I get a big client, that's when the stealing starts. And by then, I need to already have protection in place. But I can't afford protection now. So I'm stuck. I also worry about AI. I've seen AI generate art that looks like specific artists. If someone can type "in the style of Priya Sharma" and get something that looks like my work... why would they commission me?

### Q7: If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your art is protected, what would it be?

**Priya:** Something free that works globally. Not just in the US — everywhere. Something that handles the legal complexity so I don't have to understand copyright law in 50 countries. Something that watches the internet for me and tells me "hey, someone is using your art" and then actually does something about it. I don't want to learn legal processes. I want to draw.

### Q8: What do you currently do to protect your work? Any tools, services, habits?

**Priya:** Nothing. I put my name on my posts. That's it. I've thought about watermarks but they look ugly. I've thought about copyright registration but it costs money I don't have. I've thought about Terms of Service on my website but I don't even have a website yet. I know I should do something but I don't know what and I can't afford it.

### Q9: How much time and money have you spent on this in the last year?

**Priya:** Time: maybe 5 hours researching options. Money: $0. I've spent more time worrying about it than actually doing anything. That's the problem — the barrier to entry for protection is too high for someone at my level.

### Q10: What's worked? What hasn't? Why?

**Priya:** Nothing has worked because I haven't tried anything. The barrier is too high. Every option I've looked at requires either money I don't have or knowledge I don't have. It's a chicken-and-egg problem — I need protection before I get successful, but I can't afford protection until I'm successful.

### Q11: Imagine a free tool that did everything — DMCA, blockchain proof, watermarking, AI detection — all in one. What's your first reaction?

**Priya:** [excited] That's... that's exactly what I need. Free? Actually free? Because everything I've looked at costs money. If it's genuinely free and works... I'd sign up immediately. But I'd be suspicious. How is it free? What's the catch?

### Q12: What would make you trust it? What would make you suspicious?

**Priya:** Trust: if other artists I follow use it. If there's a community around it. If the creators are transparent about how it works. Suspicious: if it asks for payment information upfront. If it says "free" but has a paywall for basic features. If the website looks unprofessional. If there's no information about the team behind it.

### Q13: If you used it and it found someone had stolen your art, what would you want it to do?

**Priya:** I'd want it to send a DMCA that actually works. Not just an email — something with legal weight. And I'd want it to work globally. If someone in China steals my art, the tool should handle the China part. I don't want to have to understand international copyright law.

### Q14: What would need to be true for you to tell a friend about it?

**Priya:** I'd need to use it myself and have it work. I'd need to see it find stolen art and get it taken down. And I'd need it to stay free. If it goes paid after I've told my friends about it, I look bad. I need confidence it'll stay free before I recommend it.

### Q15: On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to sign up for this today?

**Priya:** 8. If it's genuinely free and works, I'm in. I need this. I just need to trust that it's real.

### Q16: What's the one thing that would move that number up?

**Priya:** Show me it works for artists in my situation — early-career, low income, in a non-Western country. If the success stories are all from rich American artists, I'll assume it's not for me.

### Q17: Is there anything else about protecting your art that we haven't talked about?

**Priya:** The global aspect. Most copyright tools are US-centric. But I'm in India. My clients are worldwide. The internet is global. Protection needs to be global too. If a tool only works in the US, it's useless to me.
