## INTERVIEW

### Q1: Tell me about your art. What do you create and where do you share it?

**Maya:** I do character illustration mostly — fantasy, sci-fi, some fan art. I've got about 12K followers on Instagram, a small DeviantArt presence, and I tweet my work occasionally. I do commissions on the side, maybe $500-800 a month extra. My style is kind of... anime-influenced but with more realistic proportions? I spent two years developing it and I'm finally getting recognized for it. That's actually what scares me.

### Q2: How did you get into this? What keeps you going?

**Maya:** I was doing graphic design at an agency and hated it. Quit after a year, went full-time freelance illustration. What keeps me going is... honestly, when someone messages me saying my art helped them through a hard time. That sounds cheesy but it's real. Also the money isn't bad when you build up clients. But the admin side? The business side? I'd rather gouge my eyes out.

### Q3: What's the best thing that's happened with your art this year?

**Maya:** I got hired to do a character design for an indie game. $3,000 project. Biggest single check I've ever gotten. I was so proud I cried. Then two weeks later I found my design on a Chinese print-on-demand site. On a mug. Being sold for $12. That's... that's the best and worst thing that happened this year.

### Q4: Has anyone ever used your work without permission? Tell me what happened.

**Maya:** [sighs] Yeah. So I posted this character design — it took me 40 hours, it was for a client but they said I could share it in my portfolio. I posted it on Instagram. Three weeks later someone DM'd me a link to this Chinese site. They'd taken my design, removed my signature, and were selling it on mugs, phone cases, t-shirts. The site had sold at least 200 units based on the reviews. I... I was shaking. I didn't know what to do. I'd never dealt with this before.

### Q5: How did that make you feel? What did you do about it?

**Maya:** [pauses] I felt... violated? Like someone broke into my house and stole something I made with my hands. I know it's "just pixels" but I spent 40 hours on that. I know every line, every shadow. And some company just took it and put it on a mug. I Googled "how to stop someone from stealing my art" and found DMCA stuff. I sent a DMCA takedown to the site. Got an automated response. Sent another. Nothing. Sent a third two weeks later. Nothing. I posted about it on Twitter and people were like "yeah that happens, nothing you can do." That made me feel even worse. Like this is just... normal? We're all supposed to just accept this?

### Q6: What do you worry about when it comes to your art being stolen or copied?

**Maya:** Everything. I worry about AI now. I've seen people generate art that looks exactly like specific artists' styles. If someone can type "in the style of Maya Chen" and get something that looks like my work... what's the point of me? What am I selling? I also worry about the print-on-demand stuff getting worse. And I worry about clients finding out their work is being sold on cheap products. That could ruin my reputation.

### Q7: If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your art is protected, what would it be?

**Maya:** I want something that watches the internet for my art and automatically takes it down. Like, I don't want to spend 4 hours a week Googling my own work. I want a system that does it for me. And when it finds someone stealing it, I want it to actually work — not just send an email that gets ignored. I want teeth.

### Q8: What do you currently do to protect your work? Any tools, services, habits?

**Maya:** Honestly? Nothing systematic. I sometimes add a watermark to preview images but it looks ugly and clients hate it. I have a copyright notice on my website. That's it. I know it's not enough but I don't know what else to do. Every time I look into it, it's either expensive or complicated or both.

### Q9: How much time and money have you spent on this in the last year?

**Maya:** Time? Maybe 20 hours total — researching, sending DMCAs that didn't work, worrying. Money? $0 on actual protection. I spent $55 on a copyright registration once but it felt pointless because I'd have to register every single piece individually. That's $55 × maybe 50 pieces a year = $2,750. I don't have that.

### Q10: What's worked? What hasn't? Why?

**Maya:** Nothing has worked. DMCA takedowns get ignored. Watermarks look bad. Copyright registration is too expensive per piece. I've thought about hiring a lawyer but that's $300/hour and I'd need them on retainer. I'm a freelancer making $42K — I can't afford that. The system is broken for people like me. The big studios have legal teams. I have Twitter.

### Q11: Imagine a free tool that did everything — DMCA, blockchain proof, watermarking, AI detection — all in one. What's your first reaction?

**Maya:** [leans forward] Free? Like, actually free? My first reaction is... what's the catch? Because nothing is free. If I'm not paying, they're selling my data. Or it's a free trial and then they hit me with $50/month. Or it's "free" but the good features are locked behind a paywall. I've been burned by "free" before.

### Q12: What would make you trust it? What would make you suspicious?

**Maya:** Trust... I'd need to see other artists using it. Like, if I saw three artists I follow on Twitter saying "this actually works, it found my stolen art and got it taken down" — that would make me pay attention. Suspicious? If it asks for my social security number. If it wants access to my files. If the website looks janky. If there's no information about who made it. If the reviews are all five stars with no negatives — that screams fake.

### Q13: If you used it and it found someone had stolen your art, what would you want it to do?

**Maya:** I want it to send a real DMCA — not an email, a legal document. I want it to track whether the takedown worked. If the site ignores it, I want it to escalate — maybe contact the hosting provider, or the domain registrar. And I want proof. Like a timestamped record that says "on this date, this site had your art, and here's the evidence." That way if I ever need to go to court, I have something.

### Q14: What would need to be true for you to tell a friend about it?

**Maya:** It would need to actually work. Like, I'd need to use it myself and have a success story. "Hey, I used this thing and it found my art on three sites and got two of them taken down." That's what I'd tell my friends. Not "I signed up and it looks cool." Results. I need results before I'd recommend anything. I've recommended tools before that turned out to be garbage and I looked stupid.

### Q15: On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to sign up for this today?

**Maya:** If it's genuinely free and doesn't ask for weird personal info? Maybe a 6. I'm interested but I've been burned. I need to see proof it works first.

### Q16: What's the one thing that would move that number up?

**Maya:** Show me one artist — just one — who used it and got their stolen art taken down. With proof. A screenshot of the takedown notice, the site removing the content, something real. Not a testimonial on a landing page. A real person I can DM and ask "did this actually work?"

### Q17: Is there anything else about protecting your art that we haven't talked about?

**Maya:** I think... the emotional side of it. When someone steals your art, it's not just about money. It's about someone taking something you made with your hands and not caring. It's personal. And then when you try to do something about it and the system fails you, it feels like the world is telling you "your work doesn't matter." I want to feel like someone has my back. Like there's a system that says "your art is worth protecting and we'll help you do it."
