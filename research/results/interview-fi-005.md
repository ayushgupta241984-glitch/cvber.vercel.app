## INTERVIEW

### Q1: Tell me about your art. What do you create and where do you share it?

**Sam:** I do brand illustration — brand mascots, campaign visuals, editorial illustration for tech companies. I've been doing this for nine years. I have about 20K on Instagram, 12K on Twitter, a personal site with my portfolio, and an Etsy shop where I sell prints. My clients include three Fortune 500 companies. This is a real business, not a hobby.

### Q2: How did you get into this? What keeps you going?

**Sam:** I studied graphic design, pivoted to illustration, built my portfolio, and kept going. What keeps me going is the business side, honestly. I enjoy the creative work but I really enjoy building something. I have a system — client acquisition, project management, protection. The protection part is what I want to talk about today because it's the weakest link in my system.

### Q3: What's the best thing that's happened with your art this year?

**Sam:** I got hired to create a brand mascot for a fintech startup. $12,000 project. Biggest single project ever. The mascot went viral on social media. Then, two months later, I found the mascot on AliExpress. Someone had ripped the design and was selling it as a plush toy. That's $12,000 of work being sold for $8 on a Chinese marketplace. The client was furious. I was furious. And there was nothing I could do about it.

### Q4: Has anyone ever used your work without permission? Tell me what happened.

**Sam:** Multiple times. The mascot incident was the worst because it involved a client. But I've also had my editorial illustrations reposted on design blogs without credit. My Etsy prints have been copied and sold on Redbubble. And my personal illustrations have appeared on print-on-demand sites. In nine years, I've dealt with probably 30+ incidents of theft. I have a spreadsheet tracking them all.

### Q5: How did that make you feel? What did you do about it?

**Sam:** The first few times, I was angry. Now I'm efficient. I have a system. When I find theft: 1) Screenshot with metadata. 2) File DMCA. 3) If DMCA fails, contact hosting provider. 4) If hosting fails, contact domain registrar. 5) If all fails, consider legal action. It takes about 4 hours per incident. At my rate, that's $300 per incident in time alone. Plus the emotional cost. Plus the client relationship damage when it's client work. The total cost of theft in my business is probably $5,000-8,000/year when you add it all up.

### Q6: What do you worry about when it comes to your art being stolen or copied?

**Sam:** Three things. One: client work getting leaked destroys trust. If a client pays $12,000 for a mascot and finds it on AliExpress, they're not hiring me again. Two: AI can now generate art in specific styles. I've seen AI generate work that looks like my portfolio. That's my style being cloned without my consent. Three: the scale. It used to be occasional. Now it's constant. The volume of theft is increasing and my capacity to deal with it is fixed.

### Q7: If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your art is protected, what would it be?

**Sam:** Automated, legal-grade enforcement at scale. I want a system that monitors the internet, detects theft, files legal DMCAs, tracks compliance, escalates when needed, and maintains a legal record — all automatically. I want it to work like a legal team but at the cost of a software subscription. I've done the math: a lawyer on retainer costs $3,600/year. I can justify $600/year for a tool that does the same thing.

### Q8: What do you currently do to protect your work? Any tools, services, habits?

**Sam:** I have Terms of Service on my website that prohibit unauthorized use. I register copyrights for client work ($55 per registration). I use lower-resolution images on my portfolio. I have a manual monitoring process — I Google my work monthly. I file DMCAs myself. Total system cost: $400/year in registration fees, plus about 50 hours of my time.

### Q9: How much time and money have you spent on this in the last year?

**Sam:** Money: $400 in copyright registration. Time: about 50 hours. At $75/hour, that's $3,750 in time. Total: $4,150/year. For a system that still fails regularly. That's the problem — I'm spending $4,000+ and it's still not enough.

### Q10: What's worked? What hasn't? Why?

**Sam:** Copyright registration works for legal leverage. DMCA works about 50% of the time. Manual monitoring catches theft eventually. What hasn't worked: anything automated I've tried. I've tested three "AI protection" tools. They all found false positives, missed real theft, and required more manual work than doing it myself. The technology isn't there yet.

### Q11: Imagine a free tool that did everything — DMCA, blockchain proof, watermarking, AI detection — all in one. What's your first reaction?

**Sam:** Interested but skeptical. Free is great but I need to understand the business model. If it's free, are they selling my data? Are they going to pivot to paid? I've been in this industry long enough to know that "free" usually means "we haven't figured out how to charge you yet." But the concept is exactly right. The question is execution and sustainability.

### Q12: What would make you trust it? What would make you suspicious?

**Sam:** Trust: business model transparency. Show me how you make money. Open source the core tech. Have a public roadmap. Show me the team's credentials. Suspicious: "AI-powered" without technical details. No business model page. Free with no path to sustainability. Privacy policy that's vague. No API documentation.

### Q13: If you used it and it found someone had stolen your art, what would you want it to do?

**Sam:** Auto-file legal DMCA with hosting provider AND domain registrar. Track compliance. Escalate automatically if ignored. Maintain legal documentation. And give me a simple notification: "Found on [site], filed DMCA, [status]." I don't want a dashboard. I want a notification system.

### Q14: What would need to be true for you to tell a friend about it?

**Sam:** It would need to work for me for 3+ months. I'd need to have filed multiple successful takedowns. I'd need to understand and trust the business model. And it would need to save me time — if it's more work than my current system, I won't switch.

### Q15: On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to sign up for this today?

**Sam:** 6. I'm interested. I want to understand the tech and the business model. But I've been burned by tools that promise everything and deliver nothing. Show me the goods.

### Q16: What's the one thing that would move that number up?

**Sam:** Technical documentation. Let me read the API docs. Let me understand how the blockchain proof works. Let me see the architecture. If the tech is solid, I'm in. If it's vaporware, I'm out.

### Q17: Is there anything else about protecting your art that we haven't talked about?

**Sam:** The business impact. Every incident of theft costs me $300+ in time, damages client relationships, and reduces my competitive advantage. A tool that prevents even 5 incidents/year saves me $1,500+ in direct costs, plus immeasurable relationship value. Frame the product as a business tool, not an art tool. I'm not protecting my art — I'm protecting my business.
