# CVBER — Human Validation Study Guide
## Test Synthetic Findings Against Real Artists

---

## Study Design

**Goal:** Validate the top 5 findings from 1,200 synthetic interviews against real human responses.

**Method:** Semi-structured interviews (same 17 questions from cognitive engine)

**Sample:** 5-8 real artists, 1-2 per top segment

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes each

**Format:** Video call (Zoom/Google Meet) or voice message exchange

---

## Recruitment Criteria

### Target Segments (Priority Order)
1. Freelance Illustrator (most interviews, strongest signal)
2. Photographer (high theft rate, clear pain)
3. Comic/Manga Artist (highest theft rate, most desperate)
4. Graphic Designer (highest WTP, most professional)
5. Digital Painter (strong emotional signal)

### Participant Requirements
- Active creator (posts art regularly)
- Has experienced theft OR worries about it
- Uses social media for art (Instagram, Twitter, ArtStation)
- Willing to talk for 30-45 minutes
- NOT a CVBER user (fresh perspective)

### Recruitment Channels
1. **Reddit:** r/ArtBusiness, r/DigitalArt, r/ArtistLounge, r/photography
2. **Twitter/X:** DM artists with "©" in bio or who post about theft
3. **Instagram:** DM artists with 1K-50K followers
4. **Discord:** Art servers, photography servers

### Incentive
- Offer $10-15 Amazon gift card OR
- Offer early access to CVBER when it launches
- Or just ask — many artists will talk for free about this topic

## Screening Questions (5 minutes)

Before the full interview, confirm they qualify:

1. "Do you create and share original art online?" → Must be Yes
2. "What platforms do you post on?" → Must name at least 1
3. "Has anyone ever used your work without permission, or are you worried about it?" → Must be Yes to either
4. "Have you tried any tools or methods to protect your work?" → Open answer
5. "Would you be willing to talk for 30-45 minutes about this?" → Must be Yes

---

## Interview Script (35-40 minutes)

### Introduction (2 minutes)
"Thank you for talking with me. I'm researching how artists protect their work from theft and AI. There are no right or wrong answers — I want to hear your honest experience. I'll be asking about your art, any experiences with theft, and what you think about protection tools. Can I record this for note-taking purposes?"

### Part 1: Their Art (5 minutes)
1. "Tell me about your art. What do you create and where do you share it?"
2. "How did you get into this? What keeps you going?"
3. "What's the best thing that's happened with your art this year?"

### Part 2: Theft Experience (10 minutes)
4. "Has anyone ever used your work without permission? Tell me what happened."
5. "How did that make you feel? What did you do about it?"
6. "What do you worry about when it comes to your art being stolen or copied?"
7. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your art is protected, what would it be?"

### Part 3: Current Solutions (5 minutes)
8. "What do you currently do to protect your work? Any tools, services, habits?"
9. "How much time and money have you spent on this in the last year?"
10. "What's worked? What hasn't? Why?"

### Part 4: CVBER Concept Test (10 minutes)
11. "Imagine a free tool that did everything — DMCA, blockchain proof, watermarking, AI detection — all in one. What's your first reaction?"
12. "What would make you trust it? What would make you suspicious?"
13. "If you used it and it found someone had stolen your art, what would you want it to do?"
14. "What would need to be true for you to tell a friend about it?"

### Part 5: Closing (5 minutes)
15. "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to sign up for this today?"
16. "What's the one thing that would move that number up?"
17. "Is there anything else about protecting your art that we haven't talked about?"

## What to Compare (Synthetic vs Real)

After each interview, compare against the synthetic findings:

### Finding 1: "Antivirus for Art" Concept
**Synthetic:** 68% independently used this metaphor
**Test:** Do real artists describe the ideal solution as "install and forget"?
**Record:** Exact phrases they use to describe the ideal solution

### Finding 2: Proof Before Trust
**Synthetic:** 91% demanded proof before commitment
**Test:** What do real artists say they need to see before signing up?
**Record:** Specific proof requirements (case studies, demos, trials, etc.)

### Finding 3: Free Suspicion
**Synthetic:** 78% suspicious of "free" tools
**Test:** How do real artists react to "free"? What concerns come up?
**Record:** Exact concerns and trust requirements

### Finding 4: Emotional Cost
**Synthetic:** 62% talked about emotional damage more than financial
**Test:** Do real artists emphasize emotional impact? How do they describe it?
**Record:** Emotional language and specific incidents

### Finding 5: Global Enforcement
**Synthetic:** 45% said US-centric tools are useless
**Test:** Do real artists need global protection? How do they describe the problem?
**Record:** Geographic concerns and specific examples

## Tips for Interviews

1. **Shut up and listen.** After asking a question, wait. Let them fill the silence.
2. **Probe with "Oh?"** — the two-letter question is the most powerful probe.
3. **Mirror their language.** Don't introduce jargon they haven't used.
4. **Ask for specific incidents.** "Can you tell me about a specific time?" not "Do you ever..."
5. **Don't lead.** Never ask "Isn't that frustrating?" — ask "How did that make you feel?"
6. **Record everything.** Take notes on exact phrases — these are marketing gold.
7. **Follow the emotion.** When they get emotional, lean in. That's where the insight is.
