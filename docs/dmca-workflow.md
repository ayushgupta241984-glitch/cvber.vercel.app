# DMCA Workflow

## Overview

CVBER helps creators generate DMCA takedown notices by assembling evidence and providing templates. CVBER is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

## How It Works

### 1. Detection

CVBER's reverse image search finds where your artwork appears online. Results include:
- URL of the infringing copy
- Platform hosting the copy
- Similarity score
- Screenshot of the copy

### 2. Evidence Assembly

Before filing a DMCA, CVBER assembles:
- Original file hash and timestamp
- Proof of ownership (upload date, metadata)
- AI analysis confirming the match
- Watermark verification

### 3. Template Generation

CVBER generates a DMCA takedown notice template containing:
- Your contact information
- Description of the original work
- Description of the infringing material
- Good faith belief statement
- Accuracy statement
- Electronic signature placeholder

### 4. Filing

The creator fills in their details and submits the notice to:
- The platform's DMCA agent
- The hosting provider
- Google (for search delisting)

### 5. Follow-up

CVBER tracks:
- Notices sent
- Responses received
- Takedowns confirmed
- Counter-notices (if any)

## What CVBER Does NOT Do

- File DMCA notices on your behalf
- Provide legal advice
- Guarantee takedown outcomes
- Represent you in legal proceedings

## Limitations

- DMCA applies to U.S. copyright law
- International takedowns may require different processes
- Some platforms have slow response times
- Counter-notices can restore content
- Fair use may apply in some cases

## Best Practices

1. Keep original files with metadata
2. Document your创作 process
3. File DMCA notices promptly
4. Keep copies of all correspondence
5. Consider consulting a lawyer for significant infringement
